Usage

The calculator can perform the following operations: +, -, *, /, and ^

Examples to try:

2 ^ 3

What does the ^ symbol mean?

The ^ symbol performs "exponentatian." For example:

3 ^ 1 = 3

5 ^ 2 = 5 * 5 = 25

2 ^ 3 = 2 * 2 * 2 = 8

4 ^ 4 = 4 * 4 * 4 * 4 = 256

The Challenge

This hacking challenge is designed for non-programmers who know how to use a web browser. It will be helpful if you have attended (or read) my 20-minute Intro to Hacking.

The goal of this challenge is to cause a Denial of Service. In other words, your job is to temporarily crash the web application. If you are successful, then other users across the Web will be unable to use this calculator application.

Hall of Fame

If you succeed, email me at mikegagnon@gmail.com, and explain how you DoS'ed this web application. I'll put your name here.

Rules of Engagement

  1. If you succeed in causing a denial of service, cease your attack right away. Otherwise, you will prevent others from participating in the challenge. Don't forget to email me at mikegagnon@gmail.com so that I can put your name on the Hall of Fame.
  2. You may not use a high-volume DoS attack. On other words, do not simply flood the server with a large amount of traffic. Such an attack would harm more than just this web application. Plus it's not clever enough.
  3. You may not use "sophisticated" hacking techniques, such as remote-code execution, nor may you do anything that causes persistent damage or trouble. If you don't know what that means, don't worry: you'll never accidentally perform a remote-code-execution attack. ;-)
  4. You may only harm this web application. Do not do anything that could harm any other system.
